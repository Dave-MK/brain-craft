# Mission

**Create engineers capable of solving humanity's hardest technical problems — starting with energy.**

Not "learn Python." Not "complete a course." Not "get a certificate."

## Why energy, specifically

Electric vehicles, home batteries, rooftop solar, and increasingly complex electricity grids are all expanding at once. Coordinating them — deciding when a million batteries should charge, how to route power around a failed substation, how to forecast demand well enough that utilities stop over-building generation "just in case" — is a coordination problem at the scale of the games AI has already mastered, except the board is the electricity network and the stakes are real infrastructure.

Every percentage point of efficiency gained through better coordination means generating less electricity for the same outcome. That is usually cheaper, faster, and more practical than building new power stations. This is *coordination intelligence*, not "inventing a new power source" — and it is achievable by one person learning systematically over a few years, not just by national labs.

## Why a learning platform, specifically

The fastest way to become capable of contributing to this problem is not to read about it — it's to build toward it, one real skill at a time, in an order where each skill is immediately load-bearing for the next. That requirement — "every concept must serve the mission" — is exactly what generic courses don't provide, because generic courses are built to serve many learners with many goals. BrainCraft is built to serve one learner with one goal, and generalizes only where generalizing doesn't dilute that.

## The layered technical mission

```
Layer 1: Predict   — demand forecasting, renewable generation forecasting, equipment failure prediction, price prediction
Layer 2: Think      — optimisation algorithms that use less computation, reinforcement learning for decisions, explainable recommendations
Layer 3: Act        — battery control, EV charging scheduling, data-centre workload shifting, building HVAC optimisation, solar/wind/grid balancing
```

These three layers compose into the capstone: an **AI Energy Operating System** — starting as a digital-twin simulator (no real grid access needed to begin), proving value in simulation, and only then becoming relevant to real utilities, researchers, or investors.

## Success criteria for the learner

- Knowledge retained after years, not weeks (see [`docs/01-learning-science/`](../01-learning-science/overview.md))
- Ability to solve *novel* problems, not just repeat taught patterns
- Ability to build real, working software — not slideware
- Ability to conduct research: read papers, form hypotheses, run experiments
- Ability to improve humanity's material condition, measurably, through the systems built

## Success criteria for BrainCraft-as-platform

BrainCraft succeeds if the curriculum, once built, never goes stale (see [`docs/09-content-engine/overview.md`](../09-content-engine/overview.md) and the Living Curriculum system) and if the resulting platform is good enough that other learners and researchers would want it too — not because that was the goal, but because building for one exacting learner with a real mission tends to produce something rigorous enough to generalize.
