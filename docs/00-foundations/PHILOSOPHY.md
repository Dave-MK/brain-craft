# Philosophy

## The immutable rule

> This platform exists to create builders, not consumers. Every lesson, feature, design decision, and line of code must increase the learner's ability to understand complex systems, think independently, solve meaningful problems, and create technology that measurably improves the lives of people and the health of the planet. Simplicity is preferred over cleverness, evidence over opinion, curiosity over certainty, and long-term impact over short-term engagement. Features that maximise screen time at the expense of learning are considered defects. Success is measured not by course completion, but by the learner's ability to build things that previously seemed impossible.

This is the test every feature, lesson, and design decision must pass. Nothing overrides it, including convenience, deadlines, or "every other platform does this."

## The ten non-negotiable principles

1. **Every concept has purpose.** Stated explicitly, before the concept is taught, connected to the mission.
2. **Everything builds toward the mission.** No isolated tutorials that don't feed the AI Energy OS.
3. **Nothing is memorised without understanding.** Understanding precedes abstraction, always.
4. **Theory always leads to practice.** Reading about a concept is a precursor, never the destination.
5. **Practice always leads to creation.** Guided exercises graduate into things the learner built themselves.
6. **Creation always leads to reflection.** Nothing is "done" without the learner articulating what they learned and why it worked.
7. **Reflection always strengthens retention.** Reflection is scheduled and structured, not incidental.
8. **Knowledge is interconnected.** Every concept is a node in a graph with real prerequisites — nothing floats free.
9. **Learning never ends.** The curriculum updates itself; nothing is "finished" and left to rot.
10. **Evidence over opinion.** Pedagogical and technical decisions cite research or data, not intuition or convention.

## What this rules out

- XP, streaks, or badges that exist only to maximise time-on-platform rather than retention.
- Multiple-choice-only assessment (see [`docs/05-assessments/overview.md`](../05-assessments/overview.md)).
- Generic, mission-agnostic examples ("hello world," todo apps, calculator apps).
- Frozen content that silently drifts out of date against the libraries and research it teaches.
- Cleverness in the platform's own code that trades away simplicity for the sake of impressiveness.

## What this requires

- Every lesson is built from the learning-science research in [`docs/01-learning-science/overview.md`](../01-learning-science/overview.md), not from "how other courses do it."
- Every technical claim in curriculum content is sourced and dated so staleness can be detected (see the Living Curriculum system).
- Every capstone artifact is real, working software the learner can point to — not a certificate.
