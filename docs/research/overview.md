# Research

This folder tracks the source material BrainCraft's pedagogy and technical curriculum are built on, so claims stay traceable and the living-curriculum system has something concrete to check for staleness (see [`docs/09-content-engine/overview.md`](../09-content-engine/overview.md)).

## Domains to research and track sources for

- Cognitive/learning science: active recall, spaced repetition (FSRS specifically), interleaving, dual coding, cognitive load theory, desirable difficulties, mastery learning, metacognition — feeds [`docs/01-learning-science/overview.md`](../01-learning-science/overview.md).
- Computer science fundamentals, software engineering practice.
- Machine learning, deep learning, reinforcement learning — feeds the corresponding curriculum skill folders.
- Power systems, grid operations, energy markets, digital twins — feeds `curriculum/10-power-systems/` and the capstone.
- Graph theory and optimisation — feeds `curriculum/09-graph-algorithms/`.
- HCI/UX/accessibility for educational interfaces — feeds [`docs/14-design-system/overview.md`](../14-design-system/overview.md), [`docs/15-accessibility/overview.md`](../15-accessibility/overview.md).

## Conventions

- Each tracked source gets a dated entry (paper/doc title, link, version/date accessed, what it informs).
- When a source is superseded, the old entry is kept (struck through, not deleted) with a pointer to its replacement, so the history of *why* content changed is preserved.
